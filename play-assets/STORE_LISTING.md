## Full description  (max 4000 characters)
```
TyrePulse Inspector is the field companion for fleet tyre management. It puts
inspections, checklists, meter readings, stock counts and accident reporting in
the hands of your drivers and technicians — and it keeps working when the signal
doesn't.

Built for real depots and roadsides, every entry is saved on the device first
and syncs automatically the moment you're back online, so nothing is ever lost.

WHAT YOU CAN DO

• Tyre inspections — record tread, pressure and condition per wheel position on
  a clear vehicle diagram, attach photos, and geo-tag where the check was done.

• Checklists with sign-off — complete published checklists, capture a real
  drawn signature, and route them to a supervisor for approval.

• Daily meter logs — for fleets without telematics, capture the actual odometer
  (km) and engine-hour readings with a photo of the gauge, so "current km" stays
  accurate every day.

• Tyre stock counts — do a fast daily stock-take: set the exact quantity on
  hand, see what still needs counting, and keep the store in sync.

• Accident reports — file an incident from the scene in a few taps: vehicle,
  driver, location, severity, injuries, damage and photos.

• Scan to start — scan an asset's QR or barcode to jump straight into the right
  vehicle; the app recognises codes even inside URLs or labels.

• Browse your fleet — search vehicles and tyre records, and review history on
  the go.

BUILT FOR THE FIELD

• Offline-first — capture anywhere; automatic background sync when connected.
• Fast and focused — a home screen tailored to each user's role.
• Photo evidence — camera capture built into inspections, meters and accidents.
• Secure — sign-in required, organisation-isolated data, and role-based access
  so people only see what they should.
• Multilingual — English and Arabic.

WHO IT'S FOR

Fleet operators, workshops and tyre teams who want accurate, timely field data
feeding their tyre-cost, safety and maintenance decisions — without paper.

TyrePulse Inspector is an internal workforce tool. Accounts are provided by your
organisation's administrator. See our Privacy Policy for how data is handled.
```

## Screenshots — how to get them (the honest part)

Play requires screenshots that show the **actual running app**. These must be
captured from the app itself (I can't launch the native Android app in this
environment to grab genuine screens, and faked screenshots risk rejection).

Good news: you already have the app on the **internal testing** track, so this
takes ~5 minutes on your phone with the demo account (`playdemo` / `TyreDemo!2026`).

### Required sizes (Play rules)
- **Phone:** 2–8 images, ratio between 16:9 and 9:16, each side 320–3840 px.
  Just take normal phone screenshots (e.g. 1080×2400) — they qualify as-is.
- **7-inch & 10-inch tablet:** same ratio/size rules. Capture on a tablet or an
  Android tablet emulator. *If you don't want to support tablets, uncheck the
  tablet form factors in Play so these stop being required.*

### Suggested shot list (nice, representative screens)
1. **Home** — the role-based quick-actions grid (New Inspection, Scan, Meter
   Log, Checklists…).
2. **New Inspection** — the wheel-position diagram / entry screen.
3. **Meter Log** — odometer + gauge photo (a standout, differentiating feature).
4. **Checklists** — a checklist being filled, or the signature pad.
5. **Accident report** — the incident form.
6. **Tyre Records / Scan** — the records list or the QR scanner.

Tips: use a device with clean demo data, portrait orientation, status bar tidy,
and avoid showing any real personal data.

### Optional fallback
I can run the `Take Play Store Screenshots` workflow, which logs in with the demo
account and captures the **web** dashboard at 1080×1920. ⚠️ Those show the web
app, not this native inspector UI, so Google may consider them unrepresentative —
prefer real device screenshots for the listing.
